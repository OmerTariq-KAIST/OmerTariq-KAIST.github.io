How to use your pieces cohesively

The first step is to develop your pieces. You need to move your pieces from the bottom rank (the bottom rank is where your king is). This is called development, if your pieces are in the backrow they are unable to do anything. You can learn basic openings to help with development. I reccomend learning the London system or the Italian game. These are really beginner friendly and help you use your pieces cohesively.

How to Attack

Attacking in Chess is pretty difficult. Somehow making your pieces work together is actually pretty hard. An easy way to get your pieces to work together is creating a common goal. This could either be trying to win a piece, or attacking the king. Having your pieces work together is crucial because if there isn't another piece protecting each other, the king will either be able to take or another piece will be able to take with no consequences. Here is an example of using your pieces together.
As you can see our pieces are attacking the king on the right side of the board, this is crucial to kind of gather your pieces together because how else will they work together. Looking at the arrows we can see the threats that white can make with it's pieces. We see the bishop aiming at one of the more vulnerable squares when the king castles even though that square is protected by blacks knight. In the graphic the idea they have is too push those pawns up to create more pressure on that side. Since the king is castled it'll be hard for it to escape. By pushing those pawns up you are able to attack the knight that is protecting that vulnerable square. For example after the black knight gets kicked off that square you are able to bring the queen out to H5 also attacking that vulnerable square while also putting pressure on the F7 pawn.
